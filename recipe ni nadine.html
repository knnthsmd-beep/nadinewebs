Kare-Kare

What is Kare-Kare?

Kare-Kare is a classic Filipino stew known for its rich peanut sauce, tender meat, and assorted vegetables. It is often served with bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side, balancing the dish with its salty and umami flavor.

Ingredients

  • Beef tripe / oxtail / pork leg
  • Peanut butter or ground peanuts
  • Annatto seeds (atsuete)
  • Eggplant
  • String beans (sitaw)
  • Bok choy (pechay)
  • Banana blossoms (optional)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Bagoong (shrimp paste) for serving

How to Cook It

  1. Boil the meat until tender.
  2. Sauté onions and garlic, add annatto for color.
  3. Add peanut butter or ground peanuts to create the sauce.
  4. Combine the sauce and meat, then simmer.
  5. Add vegetables and cook until soft.
  6. Serve hot with bagoong.

Why Filipinos Love It

Kare-Kare is more than food — it’s a celebration dish, commonly served during fiestas, birthdays, and holidays. Its creamy peanut flavor and unique combination of textures make it one of the most iconic Filipino meals.
